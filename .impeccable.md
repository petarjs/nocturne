## Design Context

### Users

Nocturne is built first for home-lab and self-hosting enthusiasts who want an AI agent to compose a persistent, glanceable wall display from live data. A second audience uses it as a prosumer ambient display in homes, studios, and offices. The core job is to understand system state at a glance without opening a conventional dashboard or manually arranging pixels.

### Brand Personality

Cinematic, calm, and engineered. The display should feel alive at rest, confident during normal operation, and unmistakably urgent only when the data warrants attention.

### Aesthetic Direction

A dark ambient film-interface aesthetic with tinted blacks, disciplined typography, generous negative space, quiet surfaces, and a single expressive background system. Themes may change the fantasy, but the quality floor stays consistent. Avoid rainbow data palettes, decorative gridlines, generic dashboard chrome, gratuitous glow, typewriter effects, and motion that rearranges spatial memory without cause.

### Design Principles

1. Make every paused frame look intentional enough to be a film still.
2. Preserve glanceability: one hero, stable positions, restrained supporting detail.
3. Let data drive motion and urgency; ambient motion should be noticed mainly when it stops.
4. Enforce the system through shared tokens, primitives, and archetypes instead of per-widget improvisation.
5. Keep the canvas at least 20% emptier than feels safe, while making every slot size legible and useful.
